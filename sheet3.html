Ten tips on building your resilience

Our strength and resilience will be called upon daily. Below are a few tips to help you during this challenging period.

Effects on your body

  1. Build relationships - We need to make connections with other people. Being resilient does not mean that everything rests with you.
  2. So, make sure you have good relationships with those around you.
  3. Keep in touch with family and friends, from a safe distance!
  4. Keep things in perspective - Don’t let one bad event taint your view of the rest of your life. Bad things happen, your job is to keep it in perspective.
  5. Avoid catastrophising - Don’t fall into the trap of looking at things from an ‘all or nothing’ perspective. Nothing is ever completely perfect or absolutely horrendous. Most things sit somewhere in between.
  6. See things positively - Easier said than done, but if you take a minute to think about how you choose to explain your situation you may see it from another angle.
  7. Seeing things from a ‘glass half full’ perspective does not seem as hard to manage.
  8. An ancient Greek philosopher said “People are not disturbed by things, but by the view they take of them”.
  1. Take care of yourself - Do what you can to eat well, sleep well and find some time to do something you enjoy. Be kind to yourself.
  2. Master your emotions before they manage you - Find the positives.
  3. Remind yourself that most of what you are facing is temporary, that you have overcome setbacks before and will again. Remember to focus on what you can learn from this.
  4. Keep growing – If we learn from our mistakes we get braver because life does not get easier. Life is not something that happens to us, it happens with us.
  5. Pick yourself up – As many times as it takes.
  6. You only fail if you refuse to get back up.
  7. Feeling like you can't stop worrying, or that bad things will happen if you stop worrying
  8. Keep giving – Sometimes giving to others gives us the strength to keep moving forward.
  9. Take decisive action. If you have made a decision to do something, then do it. Don’t second guess yourself. Have courage in your conviction.
