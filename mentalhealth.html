Top 10 Tips

These activities are known to help boost your mood, relieve tension, ease stress and reduce anxiety.

  1. Make connections and talk

    High stress, busy jobs and remote working can leave you missing out on the social benefits of work each day and can make you feel lonely. Schedule in time for social breaks, such as chatting with your colleagues, to allow you to share your experiences and feel part of a group. Research has found the feeling of relatedness during a social break brings a positive association with feeling positive afterwards.

  2. Reflect on the positives

    It is easy to focus on the negative input that we receive around us, which can impact our mood. Instead, try to proactively recognise the positives. Take some time each day to write down a few things you’re grateful for and your achievements from the day. This will trigger your brain to release neurotransmitters that will improve your mood.

  8. Take part in meaningful and purposeful activities

    High stress, busy jobs and remote working can leave you missing out on the social benefits of work each day and can make you feel lonely. Schedule in time for social breaks, such as chatting with your colleagues, to allow you to share your experiences and feel part of a group.   9. Reduce caffeine intake

    A few coffees in the morning can improve energy, mood, cognition, and physical performance. However, be mindful of drinking too much as it can cause headaches, anxiety and trouble sleeping.

  10. Learn to say ‘No’

    Saying no can be hard both at work and in personal environments, especially if you have a helping and caring nature. You may not want to offend or upset others or put them in a difficult position. However, saying yes all of the time can impact on your wellbeing and lead to a sense of being overwhelmed as you are taking too much on. Say no with confidence, honestly and respectfully.
